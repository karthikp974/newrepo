A brief history of Lamborghini, the iconic Italian luxury sports car brand, has been a symbol of speed, style, and exclusivity for over 60 years. From its humble beginnings to its current status as a leader in high-performance vehicles, Lamborghini's story is one of passion, innovation, and dedication. Early Years (1960s) Ferruccio Lamborghini, a successful entrepreneur, founded the company in 1963 with a vision to create high-performance grand tourers that combined speed, style, and comfort. Miura and Countach (1970s-1980s) The Miura (1966) and Countach (1974) models cemented Lamborghini's reputation for innovative design and exceptional performance, setting new standards for supercars. Modern Era (1990s-2000s) Under Audi's ownership, Lamborghini introduced the Diablo (1990), Murciélago (2001), and Gallardo (2003), further solidifying its position in the luxury sports car market.
