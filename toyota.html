The Toyota Story: A Legacy of Innovation and Reliability Toyota, one of the world's largest and most iconic automobile manufacturers, has a rich history spanning over 80 years. From its humble beginnings to its current status as a global leader in innovation and sustainability, Toyota's story is one of dedication, perseverance, and passion. Early Years (1930s-1950s) Founded in 1937 by Kiichiro Toyoda, Toyota Motor Co. Ltd. began as a spinoff from Toyoda Automatic Loom Works, Ltd. The company's first car, the Toyota AA, was launched in 1936. Post-War Era (1950s-1960s) After World War II, Toyota faced significant challenges but persevered, introducing the iconic Land Cruiser in 1951 and expanding globally. Modern Era (1970s-1990s) Toyota revolutionized the industry with the introduction of the Corolla (1966), Camry (1982), and Prius (1997), solidifying its position as a leader in quality, reliability, and innovation. 21st Century (2000s-present) Toyota continues to innovate with models like the Mirai (2014), C-HR (2016), and RAV4 (2019), focusing on sustainability, electrification, and autonomous driving. Today and Beyond With a commitment to mobility for all, Toyota aims to shape the future of transportation, building on its legacy of innovation, quality, and reliability. From its early days to its current status as a global leader, Toyota's dedication to excellence has made it a household name.
